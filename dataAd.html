REFLECTION

The lessons we tackled this midterm in our Database Administration subject served as an excellent refresher of what I learned during my second year in Information Management. Revisiting database concepts brought back familiar topics, such as creating and manipulating databases, which provided a solid foundation to build upon. Having this background made it easier for me to grasp the new lessons we explored, but it also highlighted how much more there is to learn about databases. One of the most exciting parts of this semester was discovering a new database tool, Laragon. I was amazed by its simplicity and efficiency in managing databases, and it gave me a fresh perspective on how modern tools can streamline database administration tasks.

During this midterm period, I had the opportunity to practice typing database queries, which significantly improved my skills and confidence in executing commands. I didn't not only learned to write queries but also began to understand the underlying logic and structure of database operations more deeply. This practice also helped me develop a greater appreciation for how databases function and the crucial role they play in organizing and retrieving data efficiently. Overall, this midterm has not only refreshed my previous knowledge but also introduced me to new tools, skills, and insights that will be valuable as I continue to learn and grow in the field of database management. I look forward to applying these skills in real-world scenarios and further developing my expertise in this area.
